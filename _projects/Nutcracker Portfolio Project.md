The original nutcracker size is bigger than most nutcracker's (length=20cm). So, we need to design a new nutcracker that can be shorter in length and will support the required force in order to crack a macadamia nut. 
We can solve this problem by using a linear actuator to help, specifically the IP65 Mini Linear Actuator. (Find: new dimensions)

Given: Linear Actuator max load: 112lbs
Linear Actuator stroke length: 1"
Linear Actuator closed length: 5.13"
Force to crack nut: 222.18kg
Size of nut: 2 cm

Approach to the problem: By laying the nutcracker onto the floor, I can create a Free Body Diagram and solve a moment balance to find the new nutcracker length. At the corner of the nutcracker, there are the reaction forces; the nutcracker pushes a force towards the ground (force out), and there is a force upwards at the very end of hte nutcracker (force in). I labeled the lengths of the corner of the nutcracker to the middle of the nut as l, lc is the whole length of the nutcracker, hn is the height of the nut, and hc is the height of the linear actuator.
Since the system is not moving and in equilibrium, the sum of the moments = 0. While solving the equation, I assumed that ln = 4cm and lc = 17.54cm.

This design isn't handheld anymore, so it is less intuitive to use it in the kitchen. I feel like people usually expect nutcrackers to be quick and handheld. The linear actuator is capable of compressing high amounts of force, making the person want to wear possibly protective gear, which is annoying. Overall, I would say this design is not really usable as the person who expects to use a nutcracker will deem it unnecessary and more time consuming.
